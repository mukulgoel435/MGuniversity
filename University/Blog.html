Our certificate & Online Programs for 2022

Many of our distance-learning and online degree programmes are split into courses or modules that can be studied individually. Short courses are useful for continuing professional development, refreshing your knowledge, or exploring a new area of interest.


If you later decide that you would like to continue your studies, you may be able to transfer the credit of any completed modules towards a full qualification. Modules are made up of a number of credits. One UK credit equates to a notional 10 hours of study. The member institutions that make up the University of London offer a diverse range of subjects, including: Computing, Business and Management, Law, Economics and Human Rights, and many more.


We also offer a range of free Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) that provide you with an opportunity to start studying from home to develop a personal interest, as a stepping stone to new career opportunities, or to help enhance your career as part of your continuing professional development (CPD).


Study alongside your work or personal commitments from anywhere in the world. The University's fully online degrees offer the same quality provision as equivalent on campus programmes, but with the flexibility to fit your studies around your commitments.
